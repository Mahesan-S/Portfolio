About

“Bug Shooter” is my first 3D game and represents a major step forward in my journey as a game developer. In this project, I explored many exciting Unity features and learned how to build a complete gameplay experience in three dimensions.

project details

  • Team Size : 1

  • TimeLine : 7 Week

Introduction

I implemented player controls, shooting mechanics, and enemy AI behavior to create an engaging and interactive 3D environment. Along the way, I experimented with particle effects, lighting, materials, and camera movement to enhance the overall visual quality and feel of the game. I also learned how to design realistic terrains, sculpting landscapes and adding trees and environmental details to make the world more immersive. Additionally, I implemented a teleportation system to enable smooth 3D transitions within the level, adding more depth to gameplay. To refine the visuals further, I applied post-processing effects such as bloom, color grading, and ambient occlusion, giving the game a more polished and cinematic look. For the art style, I used cartoon-themed assets for both the player and enemies, giving the world a fun, stylized personality. Developing Bug Shooter helped me gain a deeper understanding of 3D game development, optimization, and Unity’s powerful tools — making it a key milestone in my learning journey.

Animation

In my 2D game Escape the Traps, I created a complete Animator Controller using Unity’s animation state machine system. The player’s animations — such as idle, walk, jump, and death — are managed through different animation states connected by transitions. These states are controlled through code, where specific key inputs trigger animation changes. For example, pressing movement keys activates the walk animation, jumping triggers the jump state, and when the player hits a trap, the death animation plays automatically. This setup ensures smooth and responsive character movement while keeping the gameplay immersive and visually dynamic.

Full map

In my 2D game Escape the Traps, I designed the full map layout to provide a clear and engaging gameplay flow. I used a Cinemachine camera to create smooth and dynamic camera movements that follow the player naturally throughout the level. To enhance the experience, I implemented an Audio Source component for sound effects, adding impact to actions like jumping, triggering traps, and reaching checkpoints. Additionally, I used the Canvas system in Unity to display UI elements such as markers and indicators, helping players navigate and understand objectives within the game world. This combination of visuals, audio, and smooth camera control makes the gameplay feel more polished and immersive.
